Our process

How does planning for construction work?

Contact Johannes by email or phone with a description of your project, photos, any plans, your timeline, and your anticipated budget. He will get back in touch with you within 24 hours to talk to you about the scope of your work, and for you both to get a feeling for compatibility with his and your communication and personalities. He will ask for your scope of work, or your general idea, schedule a site visit within a week or two, and refer you to his designer or an in-house designer at his kitchen and bath supplier or flooring company.

Pricing the Free Estimate

Of course we offer free estimates!

Johannes will write up an estimate and you will negotiate the scope and cost of work until you are both satisfied. Pricing is either time and materials or a project bid. Small jobs are $150 for the first hour and $65 thereafter. Emergency after hours work is $150 hour. The average cost of a bathroom can be anywhere between 15-50k, kitchens 40-80k and additions 80-140k.

Once the contracts are signed and the deposit is received, your project gets scheduled into the calendar with pending start and end dates. The deposit will allow the job to commence, materials pre-ordered, subcontractors scheduled in advance, and permits acquired.

The building site

Living with construction

Construction is messy, stressful, and expensive. Make time during your project to find extra ways to relax and soothe yourself. Trust your own process. Remodeling involves thousands of decisions and this can be taxing on the brain and nerves. Most of the building process is destructive, messy, dirty, noisy, and building sites are generally unpleasant to live on.

Here are some of the ways we recommend you cope. Make space for the destruction/construction by:

  • go on vacation
  • set up a temporary or back-up living space
  • stay with family
  • camp out
  • temporarily move out

Know that when you are done, your building will be renewed and beautiful because the “doctor” has been at work.

Choosing a Contractor
